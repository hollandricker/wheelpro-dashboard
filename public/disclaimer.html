Specific risks of options trading

Before trading any option you must read and understand the OCC publication "Characteristics and Risks of Standardized Options" (the "OCC Disclosure"). It is incorporated into the WheelPro Terms of Service by reference and is mandatory reading for every options trader.

Risks specific to the strategies the Service is built around (cash-secured puts, covered calls, the wheel) include but are not limited to:

  • Assignment risk: a sold put can be assigned at any time before expiration; you may be forced to buy shares at the strike price even if the market price has fallen far below.
  • Early-exercise risk: American-style options on dividend-paying stocks may be exercised early, often around ex-dividend dates, in ways your screening criteria did not anticipate.
  • Pin risk: options whose underlying closes very near the strike at expiration can leave you with unintended overnight share exposure.
  • Gap risk: overnight news, earnings, or macro shocks can move the underlying through your strike with no chance to react.
  • Liquidity risk: options on lightly-traded names may have wide bid-ask spreads, partial fills, or no bids when you want to close.
  • Counterparty & clearing risk: while OCC clearing protects against most counterparty defaults, your broker's solvency, margin calls, and account-level rules apply on top.
  • Tax complexity: wheel-strategy outcomes (assignment, called-away shares, qualified vs. non-qualified treatment, wash-sale rules) interact in ways that may surprise you. Consult a tax adviser.
  • Concentrated capital: selling a cash-secured put requires you to set aside the strike × 100 in collateral. Concentrating capital in any single position increases idiosyncratic risk.
